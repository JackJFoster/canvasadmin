SoVS Supervisor Allocation

A web app to automate Honours project allocation in the School of Veterinary Science, University of Liverpool.

Staff can now submit their projects for 2023/24 (click above).

How does this web app work?

This online webapp replaces a pre-existing manual allocation process.

  • Students submit their preferences for project area keywords and project types.
  • An automated allocation algorithm works to optimise matches based on the preferences of all students.

Improvements

  • Staff can now keep track of projects that they have submitted.
  • Staff can re-submit previous projects with any necessary edits.
  • All staff will be allocated at least one project.
  • Unhappy with your allocated project? You can request a re-allocation.

Why can't I see a list of project titles?

In pevious years students submitted a long prioritised preference list for projects based on project titles and descriptions. This meant that only very few students received their first (or even second, third fourth or fifth) choice of project. Now, by allowing you to prioritise based on project area keywords and project types alone it is much more likely that you will be allocated a project that will meet your preferences.

Project allocation myths.

  • Students with higher average grades are more likely to be allocated their first choice of project type and area.
  • Projects are allocated on a first come first serve basis.
  • Staff are able to allocate projects to students independently of this automated process.
